VIJANA TWAWEZA (Youth We Can) is a refugee community cooperation initiative of the refugee youth of Kakuma and Kalobeyei begun in 2018.

  • We are motivated to work together to solve some of the basic problems our community faces ‚Äì in particular the problem of inadequate diet in Kakuma and Kalobeyei.
  • Our aim is to improve nutrition by increasing the diversity of domestic animals and crops to better withstand the negative effects of under nourishment and increase food availability.

Currently, Vijana Twaweza Club includes 40 refugee youths: 18 females and 22 males.

    So we are...

The camp hosts a population of nearly 218, 698 registered refugees and asylum seekers at the end of Nov., 2021. It is a multinational and multi-ethnic refugee camp, hosting refugees from different backgrounds and countries: D.R. Congo, South Sudan, Sudan, Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Somalia, Ethiopia, among others. Kakuma is semi-arid, with frequent dust storms and temperatures reaching 40 degrees regularly.

Malnutrition in the camp is a problem, especially among children under five years of age. Farmers in Kakuma do not plant enough vegetables like corchorus olitorious, cowpeas leaves, spinach, amaranth, ocra, and as result the price of vegetables on the market is very high, resulting in refugees eating more affordable vegetables - mostly yellow peas. Outbreaks of malaria, pneumonia and cholera are frequent, with high stress, age, excessive caffeine taking and substance abuse further compounding problems in the camp.

At the end of each month, the refugees receive food rations such as peas, sorghum, maize or rice, and sometimes beans (depending with the family size) from the World Food Program. In addition, the refugees receive soap and a small amount of money, which they can use to buy fish, meat, vegetables, salt, etc. in Kakuma‚Äôs 3 main markets. By the UN‚Äôs own estimates, current food rationing supplies only 60% of the recommended quantity of 2100 kcal/person/day.

We have decided to set up Vijana Twaweza Club so we can work together to ensure better nutrition for everyone in the camp. We are planing to set up this project, which will increase food availability and reduce the high prices in the market. We will focus on improving the variety of crop and animal husbandry. We will sell good quality products at reasonable prices.

Our Mission

The mission statement of VTC shall be ‚ÄúMaintaining the environment and producing adequate food.

The mission of VTC is to ensure that a biodiversity of flora and fauna is well-managed in the community and that all residents of the community have adequate access to sufficient nutrition.
